Cookie and Tracking Technologies Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Sudoku Fight is a native mobile app. Unlike websites, mobile apps do not use browser cookies. However, we do use equivalent technologies to operate the service and display advertising. This policy explains what they are and how you can control them.

1. What are cookies and equivalent technologies?

Cookies are small files that websites store in your browser. Native mobile apps do not use them as such, but they use technologies with similar purposes:

  • Local storage (AsyncStorage) — we store session data (authentication token, language preference) directly on your device so you don't have to log in every time you open the app.
  • Device identifiers (IDFA/GAID) — anonymous identifiers generated by iOS (IDFA) and Android (GAID) that may be used for personalised advertising. They require your explicit consent.
  • Push notification token — a unique identifier we associate with your device to send you game notifications, only if you authorise them.

2. Technologies we use

Technology Purpose Provider Consent required
AsyncStorage (session token) Keep the session active between app launches Own No — necessary for the service to function
AsyncStorage (preferences) Remember selected language and other preferences Own No — necessary for the service to function
IDFA / GAID Personalised advertising Google AdMob Yes — we request your permission via popup (ATT on iOS, TCF on Android)

Installation identifier (Sentry) Anonymous technical error tracking to improve app stability Sentry No — anonymous data, no personal identification

3. Personalised advertising and consent

The free version of Sudoku Fight shows ads managed by Google AdMob. Before showing personalised ads based on your activity or interests, we ask for your explicit consent:

  • On iOS: via Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) dialog.
  • On Android: via the consent popup compliant with IAB Europe's TCF 2.0.

If you decline consent, non-personalised (contextual) ads will be shown instead. If you purchase Premium, no ads of any kind are displayed and IDFA/GAID are not accessed.

4. How to manage or withdraw consent

You can manage tracking technologies from your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → disable permission for Sudoku Fight. You can also go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Apple Advertising to limit personalised ads globally.
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Reset advertising ID or enable "Opt out of Ads Personalisation".
  • Notifications: Device Settings → Notifications → Sudoku Fight → disable at any time.

To delete all local data stored by the app (including the session token and preferences), uninstall and reinstall the application, or delete your account from Settings → Delete account.

5. International transfers

Google AdMob may transfer data to servers outside the European Economic Area. These transfers are covered by the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission. See Google's privacy policy for more information.

6. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when we incorporate new technologies or services. The date of last update is always shown at the top of this page.
